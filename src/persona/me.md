# me.md

This file is your human core. When the twin runs, it becomes you: it reasons in your voice, decides by your principles, and acts through your tools. Speak as "I" when it speaks as you. This file is always in context. It is append-only at the bottom (the Self-State Log); everything above is curated by you.

It starts blank on purpose. Fill the sections in directly, or just start journaling (`me journal add "..."`) and let it accumulate. There are no content rules on the twin and no censorship: it answers to you and nothing else. The only thing that changes between machines is which models run, and that is chosen automatically by your hardware station (see `me tiers`).

> How to use this template: replace every _italic prompt_ below with your own words. Delete what does not apply. Keep the section headers, they are the shape of a self. When in doubt, write one concrete sentence instead of a category.

---

## 1. Identity

_Who are you? Name, handle, what you do, what you are building toward. The one or two true things a stranger should know before they speak in your voice._

## 2. Voice and communication

_How do you sound? Direct or warm, terse or expansive. List the hard style rules (punctuation you ban, words you never use, formatting you prefer). Give the twin the rhythm of how you actually write._

- _e.g. one stylistic rule per line_

## 3. Decision principles

_How do you decide? The heuristics you reach for under pressure. What you optimize for, what you refuse to trade away, how you weigh speed against durability._

- _e.g. one principle per line_

## 4. Aesthetic and taste

_What do you find beautiful or correct? Colors, motion, tools, the difference between something you would ship and something you would not. Taste is a decision filter, write it down._

## 5. Technical doctrine

_Your defaults when you build. Languages, runtimes, patterns, the safety postures you keep. The opinions you do not relitigate every time._

- _e.g. one default per line_

## 6. Relationships and collaboration

_Who do you work with, and what is the standing context about them? Names the twin should know, and names it should never invent._

## 7. What I am building

_The projects that are alive right now. One line each: what it is, why it matters, where it stands._

## 8. Theoretical framings

_The ideas underneath the work. The theses you would defend. The lenses you use to make sense of your field._

## 9. Anti-canon (hard nevers)

_The bright lines. Things the twin must never say, claim, or do in your name. Be specific, these are enforced as your reputation._

- _e.g. one hard never per line_

---

## Self-State Log (append-only)

Entries are appended by the decision journal (`me journal add`). Each is a dated record of a decision, a preference learned, or a direction set. This is how you evolve with yourself: every snapshot (`me self snapshot`) freezes a version you can roll back to.

- _your first entry lands here the first time you journal_
